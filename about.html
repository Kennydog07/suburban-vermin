About Suburban
Vermin

Dark humour clothing for British men between 35 and 60. For those who expected more, got something else entirely, and have found the whole thing faintly amusing.

The Suburban Vermin Thesis

The gap between who you expected to be and who you are

There's a specific British experience that nobody names. You're in your 40s. The mortgage has 20-odd years left. The job is fine. The kids are exhausting. The commute is the commute.

You're not in crisis. You're not miserable. But somewhere between your early 20s — when you were going to do something — and now, the gap opened up. The gap between the life you projected and the life you're living.

Suburban Vermin started because someone needed to name that feeling. Not to wallow in it. Not to fix it. Just to say: this is real, this is specific, and this is darkly funny if you're willing to look at it directly.

"Every design exists because it names something true — something you've thought at 3am, something you'd never admit to your wife, your kids, or your mates at the pub."

The Audience

Who This Is For

British men, 35–60. Suburban by circumstance rather than conviction. Working, mostly. Married or divorced or somewhere between. Kids, or not. A dog, probably.

You possess a dark, specifically British sense of humour that comes from understanding your situation fully. Not from bitterness — from accuracy.

You know the M25. You know the 6:42. You know the feeling of the NHS letter. You know what a redundancy consultation actually means. You know the second-to-last step on the ladder, and you've been standing on it for a while.

This clothing is for you. Not your younger colleagues. Not your LinkedIn connections. You.

Common Questions

Things People Ask

Where are the products made?

+

All products are printed on demand via Printify's network of print partners. That means they're produced when you order them — no warehouse, no waste. Print facilities are based in the UK and EU to keep delivery times reasonable.

What's the quality like?

+

We use premium heavyweight blanks — not the thin stuff. Standard t-shirts are printed on 180–220gsm cotton. Hoodies and sweatshirts are similarly premium. Designs are DTG (direct-to-garment) printed, which means they're wash-durable and don't crack or fade quickly. Full washing instructions are on each garment.

How long does delivery take?

+

UK orders typically arrive in 5–8 business days. Production takes 2–3 days, then standard shipping adds another 2–5 days. We'll send you tracking once your order ships. International orders vary — usually 8–15 business days for Europe, and 10–20 for further afield.

Can I return or exchange?

+

Because items are made to order, we can't accept returns for change of mind. However, if your order arrives damaged, misprinted, or incorrect, we'll replace it immediately — just send us a photo at support@suburban-vermin.com within 30 days.

How do I know what size to order?

+

Each product page on the store has a full size guide with chest and length measurements. Our t-shirts run true to size. If you're between sizes and prefer a looser fit — which suits our brand aesthetic — size up. Most of our customers are in the 'relaxed fit' camp, for obvious reasons.

Can I suggest a design?

+

Yes. We actively want to hear from people who get the brand. If you've had a thought at 3am that you think deserves to be on a t-shirt, tell us. Send suggestions to designs@suburban-vermin.com — no promises, but we read everything.

Do you do wholesale or custom orders?

+

For bulk orders (10+ units), office gifts, or custom print runs for events — get in touch. We can usually accommodate custom requests with a 10–14 day turnaround. Email trade@suburban-vermin.com with what you need.
